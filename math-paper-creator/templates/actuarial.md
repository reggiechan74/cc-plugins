# {title}

## Outline

### Introduction
The insurance or pension context, the risk being modeled, and the
actuarial question to be answered.
**Symbols:** none

### Population and Cohorts
Groups of policyholders, age bands, risk classes, or lines of
business. The index sets structuring the model.
**Symbols:** Set

### Mortality or Hazard Model
Survival functions, force of mortality, or loss frequency and
severity distributions. The probabilistic foundation.
**Symbols:** Parameter, Variable

### Cash Flow Structure
Premium inflows, benefit outflows, and their timing. Define the
financial flows associated with the insurance contract.
**Symbols:** Parameter, Expression

### Reserve Calculation
The liability valuation — prospective or retrospective reserves,
present values, and discount factors.
**Symbols:** Expression, Objective

### Risk Measures
Quantification of uncertainty — Value at Risk, conditional tail
expectation, risk margins, or capital requirements.
**Symbols:** Expression, Constraint

### Experience Adjustments (optional)
Credibility weighting, bonus-malus systems, or assumption updates
based on emerging experience data.
**Symbols:** Parameter, Expression

### Conclusion
Summary of the actuarial model, key assumptions, regulatory
considerations, and sensitivity to assumptions.
**Symbols:** none
